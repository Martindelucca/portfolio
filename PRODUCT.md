# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Local business owners, independent professionals, and small teams in Argentina who need a clearer online presence or a simple operational tool without dealing with technical complexity. They are often running the business day to day, answering customers through WhatsApp, selling through Instagram or word of mouth, and using spreadsheets, notebooks, or disconnected tools to manage sales, stock, and cash.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio presents Martin De Lucca's services for local businesses: professional landing pages connected to WhatsApp, simple sales / stock / cash systems, and first-step online presence improvements. Its job is to turn unclear digital presence and operational disorder into concrete, understandable next steps that generate better inquiries, more trust, and simpler daily work.

Success looks like a visitor quickly understanding what Martin does, recognizing their own business problem, trusting that the solution will be practical rather than oversized, and starting a WhatsApp conversation or contact form submission with useful context.

## Brand Personality

Clear, practical, trusted.

The voice should feel direct, grounded, and useful to non-technical business owners. It should explain digital work in business language, not developer jargon. The emotional goal is confidence: the visitor should feel that the project will be handled with care, without corporate distance or unnecessary complexity.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS and startup landing-page cliches: fake metrics, inflated promises, abstract productivity claims, and hero sections that could belong to any software company. Avoid cold corporate styling that feels distant from local businesses. Avoid generic developer portfolio tropes that center the technology stack more than the client's business problem. Avoid over-technical visuals, decorative dashboards, and complexity used as proof of expertise.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the business problem, not the technology.
2. Make every next step obvious, especially WhatsApp contact paths.
3. Use plain, specific Spanish copy that earns trust without hype.
4. Show practical proof through real examples and concrete outcomes.
5. Keep the interface simple enough to match the promise of simple tools.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Maintain readable contrast in light and dark themes, preserve keyboard access for navigation, menus, forms, and disclosure controls, support reduced motion, and keep responsive layouts usable on small mobile screens. Copy should remain understandable for non-technical visitors and avoid unexplained jargon.
