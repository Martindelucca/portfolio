#### Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: This does not read as disposable AI slop overall. The strongest decisions are business-first copy, row-based services instead of generic card grids, real examples, and direct WhatsApp paths. The places that still smell generated are the repeated tiny uppercase section kickers, the 01/02/03 numbering cadence, and decorative hero orbs that do not carry business meaning. The page is credible; it just needs fewer default landing-page tells and stronger accessibility discipline.

**Deterministic scan**: `detect.mjs --json index.html` returned 3 findings.

- `gray-on-color`, warning, `index.html:374`: reported `text-zinc-950 on bg-cyan-700`. This is likely a state-combination false positive because the light state is white text on cyan hover and the dark state is dark text on cyan-200 hover. The underlying area still deserves attention because the submit button breaks the green contact-action system.
- `single-font`, warning, `index.html:1`: likely false positive. The page loads Outfit and JetBrains Mono, and the CSS uses both.
- `numbered-section-markers`, advisory, `index.html:0`: partially valid. The process `01/02/03` is a real sequence; service `01/02` is less earned because those are options, not steps.

**Visual overlays**: Browser automation is not available in this session, so no reliable user-visible overlay was injected. Fallback signal used: source review plus deterministic CLI detector.

#### Overall Impression

The page is strategically clear and much stronger than a generic developer portfolio. It talks to business owners, not engineers. The biggest opportunity is to harden the conversion layer: make every contact action semantically consistent, accessible, and unmistakably primary without relying on low-contrast green buttons or repeated template markers.

#### What's Working

- The copy is the best asset. Lines like “Sin sistemas enormes, sin vueltas” and the diagnosis section translate technical services into business anxiety and relief.
- The service-row pattern is a good brand choice. It avoids identical card grids and makes the two offers feel concrete.
- The proof section uses real screenshots and problem/action/result copy, which builds trust faster than technology badges would.

#### Priority Issues

**[P1] Primary CTA contrast fails WCAG AA**

**Why it matters**: The main green CTA color `#16a34a` with white text measures 3.30:1. Most button text is around 14px bold, so it needs 4.5:1. The service detail color `#a1a1aa` on white measures 2.56:1. These are not edge cases; they affect the primary conversion path and service comprehension.

**Fix**: Darken the base action green to the current hover green `#15803d` or another green that reaches at least 4.5:1 with white. Move service details from `#a1a1aa` to `#52525b` or a hue-related darker muted color. Audit placeholder and helper text too.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable audit index.html`

**[P1] Contact flow lacks robust feedback and recovery**

**Why it matters**: The form prevents native submission, sends async fetch, then only writes a status message. There is no pending state, no disabled submit, no `aria-live`, no field-level errors, and no protection against double-submit. For a lead form, a user must know whether the message is sending, sent, failed, and what to do next.

**Fix**: Add pending state text, disable the submit button while sending, set `aria-live="polite"` or `role="status"` on the status node, add `autocomplete` / `inputmode="tel"`, and make the fallback path to WhatsApp explicit on error.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable harden index.html`

**[P2] CTA hierarchy is fragmented**

**Why it matters**: The page uses green WhatsApp pills, black service CTAs, underlined case CTAs, a black submit button, a header review CTA, a final CTA, and a floating WhatsApp button. That gives users many paths, but it weakens the design system rule that green means contact.

**Fix**: Define one CTA hierarchy: primary contact = green, secondary proof/navigation = neutral/cyan, tertiary text links = underlined. Make service CTAs and form submit obey that hierarchy. Keep one primary CTA per section.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable polish index.html`

**[P2] Repeated section kickers and number markers feel like scaffolding**

**Why it matters**: Almost every section uses the same small uppercase mono kicker. Services and process both use `01/02/03` style markers. The process numbers are earned; the service numbers are less so. This is one of the few places the page can look AI-generated.

**Fix**: Keep labels where they clarify navigation, but vary section cadence. Replace service numbers with offer names, icons only if functional, or stronger row hierarchy. Keep process numbers because order matters there.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable typeset index.html`

**[P2] Hero visual energy is generic compared with the business promise**

**Why it matters**: The hero copy is specific, but the visual treatment is abstract orbs and text. That is safer than bad illustration, but it misses a chance to show “clarity for local business” immediately.

**Fix**: Replace or reduce decorative orbs with a concrete proof-oriented module: WhatsApp inquiry flow, before/after presence checklist, or a compact “negocio desordenado → consulta clara” visual. Keep it lightweight and real, not a fake SaaS dashboard.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable bolder index.html`

#### Persona Red Flags

**Jordan, first-time local business owner**: The page answers “what do you do?” quickly, but the number of contact labels can blur the first decision: “Revisión gratis,” “Consultar por mi negocio,” “Quiero una página web,” “Consultar por sistema,” “Pedir revisión gratis.” Jordan may wonder which one is safest if they are not sure what they need.

**Sam, accessibility-dependent user**: Contrast fails on key text/button combinations. The form status is not announced with `aria-live`, and pending submission is not communicated. Keyboard access is mostly preserved, but the lead-generation flow needs stronger accessible feedback.

**Casey, distracted mobile user**: The floating WhatsApp button helps, but the page has many CTAs and the form still asks for typed details. Casey benefits from the service select, but the WhatsApp path should remain the dominant thumb-friendly route and should not compete with a lower-contrast submit button.

**Riley, stress tester**: Riley will double-click submit, try slow network, paste long WhatsApp numbers, and expect the form to recover. Current behavior does not disable pending submits or explain field-level problems; it only reports a generic send error.

#### Minor Observations

- `.section-title` uses `letter-spacing: -0.045em`, which violates the documented floor of `-0.04em`. It is close, but tightening is exactly the kind of small typographic defect that accumulates.
- `.op-map` and related CSS appear unused. Remove stale patterns before they become accidental design vocabulary.
- `service-detail` is visually too quiet for useful detail. If the copy matters enough to be on the page, it must be readable.
- The FAQ is useful, but “Ver más preguntas” nests details inside details. Native behavior is acceptable, but the extra nesting should be tested with keyboard and screen reader flow.
- External link icons and WhatsApp SVGs should be consistently `aria-hidden="true"` when adjacent text already names the action.
